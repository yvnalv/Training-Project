MPN Guideline

Most Probable Number reference for 3-3-3 tube method

What is MPN?

The Most Probable Number (MPN) method estimates the concentration of viable microorganisms in a sample using serial dilutions and presence/absence detection. It is widely used for coliform bacteria testing in food and water safety.

The 3-3-3 Tube Method

Nine tubes are inoculated across three dilution levels — three tubes per dilution. A yellow / no bubble result indicates a positive tube (microbial growth detected). A clear / bubble result is negative.

Interpretation Guide

Safe
< 3 MPN/g
Below detection threshold. Acceptable for consumption.
Low Risk
3 – 20 MPN/g
Low contamination. Monitor and re-test recommended.
Moderate
21 – 110 MPN/g
Elevated contamination. Further investigation required.
High Risk
> 110 MPN/g
High contamination. Sample unsafe. Action required.
